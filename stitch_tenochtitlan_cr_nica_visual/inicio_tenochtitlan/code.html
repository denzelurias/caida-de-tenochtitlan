Chapter II

The Arrival of the Spanish

In 1519, Hernán Cortés and his expedition arrived on the Gulf Coast. Their march inland was marked by shifting alliances, profound misunderstandings, and the introduction of devastating new diseases.

The encounter between Moctezuma II and Cortés remains one of the most debated and pivotal moments in history, a collision of two entirely distinct worlds.

Chapter III

The Final Siege

"Broken spears lie in the roads; we have torn our hair in our grief."

After the expulsion of the Spanish during the Noche Triste, Cortés returned with a massive coalition of indigenous allies, primarily the Tlaxcalans. The brutal 93-day siege, exacerbated by a devastating smallpox outbreak, culminated in the fall of Tenochtitlan on August 13, 1521.
