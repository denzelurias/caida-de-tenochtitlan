Artifacts & Codices

A curated collection of material culture and manuscript evidence from the era of the conquest, offering dual perspectives on the fall of Tenochtitlan.

Manuscript

The Florentine Codex

Compiled by Bernardino de Sahagún, this 16th-century ethnographic research project offers vital indigenous perspectives on Nahua culture and the conquest.

Monolith

Piedra del Sol

Often called the Aztec Calendar Stone, this massive basalt sculpture details the complex Mexica understanding of time, cosmology, and previous eras of creation.

Armor

Morion Helmet

A symbol of the conquistador, this steel headpiece offered protection but was swelteringly impractical in the Mesoamerican climate.

Weaponry

Obsidian Blades

Razor-sharp volcanic glass embedded in wooden clubs (macuahuitl), capable of devastating trauma despite shattering against steel.
